My name is Zhewei Hu. I am {{ myage }} years old. I was born and grew up in Cixi, the city below Shanghai.

In 2016 Spring semester, I received the degree of Master of Science in Computer Science from North Carolina State University. My master thesis is "Scaffolding the Grading Process for Peer Review Assignments: A Pluggable Reputation Web-Service Approach". I am working as a Software Engineer Intern at Offers.com at Austin, Texas during this summer. And I has already been admitted to the PhD program in the Computer Science department of North Carolina State University.

I started to "play" with Ruby and Rails in Nov. 2014, so it is more than one year now. Since that time, I began to work on an open source project named Expertiza.

Expertiza is a web application supported by NSF written by Ruby on Rails framework where students can submit and peer-review learning objects. I contributed more than 450 commits to Expertiza master branch on GitHub. And I designed and deployed several key features, such as multi-part rubrics, team lazy initialization, automatic review mapping strategy, student suggested topics, etc.

I am also familiar with RSpec testing framework. Recently, I refactored and merged several pull requests to increase the test coverage from 28% to 45%. And I am also planning to use Rubocop auto-correction functionality to enforce ruby coding guidelines.

Last semester, I am a research assistant of Expertiza group. My job focuses on maintain the system and do research related to education data mining. I have already jointly published 2 papers.
